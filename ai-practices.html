How I use agentic models to work faster and think deeper

Agentic models are embedded across my entire workflow — from the first brief to handoff. I use them to compress research, explore multiple directions early, and prototype interactions quickly so stakeholders can react to something real rather than a concept.

The goal isn't to replace thinking — it's to spend less time on the mechanical and more time on the decisions that actually matter.

01

Why agentic models stay in the loop

Faster learning loops

I use agentic models to compress the "figure it out" phase — summarising context, surfacing edge cases, and generating directions to discuss early rather than late.

Efficiency and productivity, not shortcuts

The goal is always efficiency and better output — not cutting corners. Agentic models help me do more with the same hours and stay in a higher-quality thinking mode longer.

Human judgment stays central

Models draft; I decide. What to prioritise, simplify, ship now, or leave for later still comes from me — and that's exactly how it should be.

How I keep it grounded

  • Start with users, goals, and constraints
  • Treat model output as a draft, not truth
  • Cross-check critical claims (especially compliance)
  • Explore multiple options before converging
  • Design for the system you actually have
  • Document decisions and rationale for handoff
02

Workflow at a glance

1️⃣

Frame & constrain

Define problem, success metrics, risks, constraints. If scope isn't clear, agentic models won't help.

2️⃣

Scan & seed concepts

Use models to scan patterns, regulations, and comparables; translate into 2–3 directions that fit scope.

3️⃣

Prototype fast

Prototype interactions quickly with Cursor and Figma Make. Check components and states so concepts stay real.

4️⃣

Validate & decide

Walkthroughs with stakeholders and eng; keep the winner, tighten, hand off cleanly.
