Redesigning onboarding to improve identity accuracy at Doctor Anywhere

Redesigned Doctor Anywhere’s onboarding end to end — from fragmented, multi-flow entry points to a single, identity-first experience. The work focused on simplifying entry, improving data accuracy, and delivering a scalable onboarding foundation across B2C, B2B, and direct consult users and as a result we have verified identity data for 1M+ users.

01

Background

When I joined Doctor Anywhere in 2023, the product wasn’t a single app but an ecosystem. Public users accessed teleconsultations through the main app, while employee benefits users came in through multiple white-label apps tied to insurers. Identity, login methods, and memberships lived in different systems. As the business scaled, this setup became hard to maintain, and the decision was made to consolidate everything into one app. Onboarding became the critical layer responsible for serving public users, corporate members, and Direct VC users — while also meeting Singapore MOH compliance. This shift happened incrementally, and with each step, onboarding carried more responsibility than before.

Product ecosystem

Where multiple user groups enter

02

Process

Phase 1 focused on consolidation. We introduced separate entry points for different user types, added KYC before consultations, and shipped flows that were functionally complete. Usability tests passed and users moved forward, but real usage revealed deeper issues: users entered the wrong flows, benefits failed to appear, consultations broke due to incorrect personal details, and operations teams stepped in to fix identity gaps manually. The core issue wasn’t usability — it was confidence. We allowed users to progress before the system truly knew who they were. I reframed the problem from “how do we help users move faster” to “how can the system confidently understand identity early.” This led to an identity-first pivot: a single entry point for all users, earlier identity verification (with Singpass as a trusted source), and memberships or benefits attaching only after identity was confirmed. Much of the work involved aligning engineering, product, compliance, and insurers — balancing user experience with MOH standards, operational realities, and long-term scalability.

Outcome

Phase 2 corrected the sequencing issues exposed in Phase 1. Identity is now established before care or benefits depend on it, reducing wrong-flow starts, failed consultations, and downstream confusion. Users no longer self-classify upfront, personal data quality improved, and onboarding aligns more closely with patient and operational workflows. The rollout brought 66,000 B2B users into the unified app while maintaining uninterrupted corporate onboarding. Identity verification started at 0% adoption and, within five months of launch, 76% of users verified via Singpass, with over 1M users verified overall. This demonstrated that an identity-first approach could scale across public and corporate use cases without blocking access. The biggest takeaway for me was that onboarding isn’t just a funnel — it’s a system boundary where trust, access, and compliance are set for everything that follows.
