AI Practice Workflow Cursor + GPT

How I use AI to speed up design decisions (and ship with less rework)

AI is part of my workflow from the first brief to handoff. I use it to quickly scan context, explore multiple directions, and prototype interactions early—so stakeholders can react to something real. I also use code-aware tools like Cursor to sanity-check feasibility in complex systems, even when I’m not the one merging code.

AI supports my end-to-end design process: clarify requirements, frame the problem, run quick scans, prototype fast, iterate, align scope and intent, and hand off production-ready designs—all while keeping design rigor intact.

01

Why AI stays in the loop

Faster learning loops

I use AI to compress the “figure it out” phase—summarising context, surfacing edge cases, and generating a few directions to discuss early.

Feasibility earlier

Legacy risk: clean designs that can’t ship. Cursor/code-aware checks validate constraints early—without pretending everything can be rebuilt.

Human judgment stays central

AI drafts; I decide. What to prioritize, simplify, ship now, or leave for later still comes from me.

Guardrails

  • Start with the brief: users, goals, constraints
  • Treat AI output as a draft, not truth
  • Cross-check critical claims (esp. compliance)
  • Explore multiple options before converging
  • Keep feasibility in mind (design for the system you have)
  • Document decisions + rationale for handoff
02

Workflow at a glance

1️⃣

Frame & constrain

Define problem, success metrics, risks, constraints. If scope isn’t clear, AI won’t help.

2️⃣

Scan & seed concepts

Use AI to scan patterns, regulations, comparables; translate into 2–3 directions that fit scope.

3️⃣

Prototype in Cursor

Prototype interactions fast. Use Cursor to check components/APIs/states so concepts stay real.

4️⃣

Validate & decide

Walkthroughs with stakeholders/eng; keep the winner, tighten, hand off cleanly.

Important: In complex legacy systems, being code-aware reduces risk. I use tooling to stay realistic, not to skip design.

03

How I use GPT and Cursor

Market signals in minutes

I use GPT (with browsing) to scan patterns, competitor moves, and rules—then distill a one-pager: what matters, what’s risky, what to test first.

Feasibility inside code

Cursor helps me see how the product is built today—components, data models, constraints—so designs stay realistic in a legacy environment.

Variants and microcopy

AI generates flow options, empty/error states, and microcopy so we can choose the clearest approach before polishing.

04

Practices in action

Storybook built with Cursor

Data-aware prompts

For data/compliance work, I use prompt guardrails and source-checking—AI speeds me up, but trust comes from validation.
